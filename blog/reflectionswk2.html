Learn HTML and CSS

What obstacles did you come up against? How did you overcome them?

My ego, while coding the blog project I wanted it to sport a lot of elements commonly found on most sites. I was really OCD and set the bar too high. I was silly enough to try and restart three times. Didn't ask for help. Who knew a navbar with a non traditional configuration inluding a dropdown annd some other features would be so hard. Any-who, I reacessed my goals for the projects and scaled back my plans. Because I went over time wise and felt like I wasn'ty moving forwards. I changed how I was thinking about it as a whole. What is the task I have been given? Go back to the Dev Academy Handbook and following the instructions given. I assured myself I can always come back and tinker with the project later when I have more time and actually what I have made thus far isn't bad. The obstacle I tackled with the most was myself, my OCD, if I can control this task and make it perfect then I'm safe and everything will be okay. That relates to me having PTSD and my inner child feeling a lack of safety or control in life generally and so I try to find that feeling in what I do day to day. Thing is, I know perfect isn't achieveable for so many reasons and it's all actually about the jounery, learning and growing. Not about creating the perfect static blog site from CSS, HTML and a little JS. I challenged myself to implement a JS element into this project thus far and actually it worked. It's in the header via the .sticky class and causes a sticky navigation bar effect where the navbar follows you down the page as you scroll and changes in appearance through that transition.

What are of you proud of from this challenge?

The project as a whole and the JS element I have added in. Defiantly not my timing.

Add HTML and CSS to your Blog Challenge

Write a small story about HTML and CSS and their relationship. You could personify elements or speak from your own personal experience. The story should capture insight about the nature of HTML and CSS.

Mr. HTML is a structured man, he sets our frameworks in this community that contain raw content in their origional form. He doesn't worry about the look of things, he focuses on functionality and order.

Ms. CSS is a creative lass, she takes the work done by Mr. HTML and smartens it up. Colour, spacing, animations effects and more. She is a graphic designer of a soul and brings Mr. HTMls work to life. From drab and black and white to colour, forms and interactivity.

Some think HTML and CSS aren't real programming languages, and compared to JaveScript or C#, they aren't. But without them web development wouldn't be the same.

Any moments of surprise or joy?

Yes, when I got the JS working :)

If HTML and CSS were animals, which animals do you think they would be? Why?

HTML would be a cow, black and white, what you see is what you get. Drab, serves a function. Important for the function of society as we know it.

CSS, would be a unicorn with rainbow farts. Because of it's endless possibility to bring the drab alive.
