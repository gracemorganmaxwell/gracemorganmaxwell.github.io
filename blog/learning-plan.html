What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I want to be able to solve complex code based problems and do full stack projects myself. I would like to go down one of two paths that benefits from the study I’m doing today. One is a programmer (any type, not sure whether front or back end is more enjoyable), the second is a Business Analyst.

My goal beyond that which I’ve stated for dream role is to find a workplace which has a great culture, which believes in flexible working hours, developing their staff and being family orientated. They will use sustainable practices, be community minded and only take on projects or clients that are adding value to the world, no Donald Trumps thanks.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning:

I find that time, time tracking, information tangents or going down rabbit holes are something I need to be careful with, they can end-up being huge time sinks, it’s defiantly an area I’m trying to improve in currently. On one side you can say I’m good at research, I have an analytical mind and inquisitive mind. Which is great for problem solving. One the limitations side, it depends on the context and balance is important. Staying on task is important and going off topic is going to do me no favours.

This is all connected to some OCD I have around perfectionism, with doing study or work and the product of it needing to be amazing. I love hearing praise, who doesn’t. Even though I’m fully aware perfection is not a status one can actually achieve. The OCD is linked back to me liking to be in control of my environment and anything I do within it, as part of my trauma based PTSD.

It’s silly, I know we are learning and my first project is fine looking like something out of the 90’s. But I’d much rather it look amazing and feel as though the 90’s option isn’t good enough for the public eye, that I’ve failed. But deep down, I am aware for now it’s good, and actually with time and practice I’ll be able to create projects that do look amazing. Having an eye for detail, caring about the quality of my work is a strength.

I like to help others and I am a people pleaser, saying no to others can take my focus away from study mentally and, or physically. Staying present in the moment with my tasks is something I’m working on also, my Cognitive Behavioural Therapist taught me a mental strategy where you “reset, refocus and just be present.” It’s a mindfulness-type technique, but it takes a while to 100% retrain your brain my therapist said, so takes a lot of practice. Each day.
