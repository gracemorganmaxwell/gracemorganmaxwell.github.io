Identity, Values and Strengths Exploration

Sprint 2

22 November 2021

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

Integrity, authenticity and balance are essential to me. It's so true what they say about 'first taking care of yourself, before trying to help others, to make sure you have good life balance. But also 'treat others, how you would like to be treated' and 'always trying to put your best foot forward' and naturally to 'learn from your mistakes'. Could I have included any more cheesy cliches? My apologies. They roll off the tongue so easily and communicate an idea effectively in such a nice concise phrase.

Growing up, I always found it hard to say 'no' to others, and I always wanted to keep everyone around me happy. I'm working on it, of course. But I have PTSD, and a part of that is I believe I've been a people pleaser most of my life, something to do with wanting to belong to a group, family, organisation or team. Whatever the flavour of the month is. These experiences compounded over the years have shaped me regarding my character and my conduct with others in all walks of life. So now I try to say 'can I sleep on that' or a straight-up 'no' if I have enough spinning plates I'm trying to balance at the time.

I took that 16 personalities test by Myers Briggs and got the result of Protagonist. This result was quite relatable for me; I always see potential in others. Ideas sprout in my mind of how they could grow their potential further. I've always sported a growth mindset set for myself and others around me. I want the best for everyone, for them to be happy. In turn, I get joy from seeing others grow. Sounds cheesy, I know. But helping others, purely that is meaningful for me, its a purpose to get me out of bed in the morning and keep me accountable to my word. These days it's my daughter mostly, our pets (two dogs, two cats) and close friends and family, plus the odd bit of volunteering or contracting.

Previously I worked for a telecommunications retail service provider offering internet and mobile services to the residential and small business markets. I did online admin and customer service, which was excellent until sales KPIs came into it. Through moving sideways to customer-centric roles, I escaped sales and went on to enjoy my job in a digital space for many years, gaining responsibilities in social media management. Alas, all good things come to an end, especially with upper management needing to make cutbacks. Something I didn't realise about mental health conditions like PTSD back then is sometimes we don't want to acknowledge something happened to us because then we need to face it and deal with it. I started my rehabilitation through ACC, working various roles on and off over the last couple of years while raising my daughter.

I'm attending Dev Academy because I love to help others, I enjoy the challenge of problem-solving, and I love dabbling in tech. Through gaining the right skills during Foundations + Bootcamp, I know I'll feel ready to hit the ground running, find an opportunity and join a team where I can make a difference.
